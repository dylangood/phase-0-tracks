Mom's Strawberry Bread

My mom used to make me this strawberry bread for Christmas every year when I was a kid. It's delicious and moist, and with all the strawberries, it doesn't need as much sugar as some other dessert breads. When I grew up and moved to California, I started making it around the holidays, too.

It makes 6 mini loaves, perfect for a light dessert after a hearty family meal. They can be easily frozen and defrosted, and the cute little loaf tins can also be decorate simply with ribbons and celophane. They make excellent spontaneous gifts or party favors.

Ingredients

  • frozen whole or sliced strawberries
  • vegetable oil
  • flour
  • sugar
  • cinnamon
  • salt

Procedure

  1. Set oven temperature to preheat at 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Combine the frozen strawberries with the oil and sugar.
  3. Set them aside to thaw and macerate while you work on the dry ingredients.
  4. Sift together the flour, cinnamon, and salt.
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the wet, stirring moderately using the muffin method.
  6. Pour into 6 mini loaf pans, arrayed on a cookie sheet.
  7. Bake at 350 degrees for 45 minutes, or until a knife comes away clean.
  8. Set aside to cool for at least an hour before freezing.
